Founder ownership is a strength (alignment, long horizon) and a risk (key-person, entrenchment) in equal measure.

04Competitive Positioning & Moat

Wide moat — Launch

A durable cost-and-cadence monopoly. Reusability gives SpaceX a structural $/kg advantage no Western competitor has matched; national-security launch and NASA crew/cargo entrench it. Erosion vector: Starship execution missteps, or a credible reusable competitor (Blue Origin) at scale — both multi-year, low-probability near-term.

Narrowing-to-wide moat — Starlink

First-scaled LEO broadband: network of inter-satellite lasers, spectrum priority, and a manufacturing lead. Moat source is scale + spectrum + capital intensity. Erosion vector is real and named: Amazon Kuiper and state-backed constellations, plus the ARPU erosion the data already shows. Pricing power is unproven at the consumer tier.

Porter, briefly. Supplier power low (vertical integration). Buyer power rising in consumer broadband (commoditizing). New-entrant threat high in capital terms but gated by spectrum and launch access — which SpaceX itself controls. Substitutes (terrestrial fiber/5G) strong in dense geographies, weak in the underserved markets Starlink targets. Net: a wide moat in launch, a contested but leading moat in connectivity.

05Proprietary Technology Assessment

Genuinely proprietary, not a commodity stack with a logo:

  • Booster reusability — the defining cost lever; a decade-plus operational lead and a flywheel (each flight compounds reliability data).
  • Laser-mesh constellation — inter-satellite optical links and vertically-integrated user terminals; a 10M-subscriber data/network advantage that compounds with density.
  • Starship — fully-reusable super-heavy lift; if it works, a step-change in $/kg that opens new markets (mass constellation refresh, point-to-point, lunar/Mars). High technical risk; enormous optionality.
  • AI flywheel (xAI) — the bull-case claim is a data (X) × compute × distribution (Starlink) loop. Unproven; the defensibility of frontier models is the most contested question in technology. We assign it option value, not franchise value.

R&D is heavy and largely expensed; the build-out shows up as the ~$19.6B investing outflow, not the income statement. Third-party dependence (GPU supply for AI; spectrum/regulatory for Starlink) is the principal platform risk.

10Intrinsic Valuation — DCF & Reverse-DCF

At a 30% FCF margin that is ~$340B of revenue — ~18× FY25 — requiring a ~27% revenue CAGR sustained for 12 years. SpaceX could become that company. Forward DCF. Because the entity is FCF-negative today with terminal value dominating, a single consolidated DCF is low-confidence; we use it only to triangulate the SOTP.
